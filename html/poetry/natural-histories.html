NATURAL HISTORIES (1989)


Schizotist

God! I'm a repetitious bore:
it's I, me, my, mine all the time -
but this too I have said before.

I love myself! How I adore
the chime of every ego-rhyme!
God! I'm a repetitious bore.

I'd roll about for evermore,
pigging it in the ego-slime;
but this too I have said before.

Listen to me! You'll soon deplore
the egotistical sublime!
God! I'm a repetitious bore.

I scratch my ego like a sore,
a villain in a pantomime;
but this too I have said before.

I'm the first person's troubadour!
My life's an ego-paradigm!
God! I'm a repetitious bore.
But this too I have said before.

- Lawrence Normand in Poetry Wales

"The largeness of Poole's cosmic perspectives gives to the best of his love poems an impressive metaphysical dimension."
- Glyn Pursglove in New Welsh Review

"…these poems show skill and delicacy, and a fascination with paradox, as in "Air, an aria…"
- Katie Jones in Planet
