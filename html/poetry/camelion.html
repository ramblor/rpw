CAMELION (2012)


Camelion is a thematic collection of poems in personae. Speakers range from historical figures through fictional characters to animals and inert objects.

Shadow

I'm the dark side of you
that's visible in sunlight.

You pretend not to know me,
ignore your parasite

while I orbit about you,
a flat satellite,

dream of tangibility,
a third dimension of height

till, deftly, I melt into you
as day turns to night.

Then you melt into me.
Now my wrong is your right.

From reviews of Camelion

"Camelion may be a 'collection of poetic fictions' as the blurb tells us, but they are fictions rooted in historical figures. Nero, Tut-Ankh-Amon, Li Po, Zahir-ud-Din Muhammad Babur, Giordano Bruno, Alexander Selkirk, Kuchuk Hanem, Corvo, Hitler, Helmut Kohl, Jason, Electra, Delius, Peter Warlock. The list isn't complete, but at random it presents a feast of stories as rich as Herodotus…

There is an intellectual brilliance and playfulness to Richard Poole's imagination which may only appeal to a certain kind of reader – it would have infuriated Larkin – but rewards repeated readings…

The philosopher's nothingness – Sartre here – remains the unifying theme throughout, metaphorically brought to life in the closing poem, 'Nothing', with the wonderful line: 'I'm the keyhole that invited the invention of the key'. There is both humanity and humour here, the metaphor brilliantly revealing Poole as poet before dry theorist."
- William Bedford in Acumen
