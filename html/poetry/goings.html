GOINGS AND OTHER POEMS (1978)


Abandoned Slate Quarry, Nantlle
1.

Carved bodily out of the mountain's dark bone,
the emptiness is absolute.

Bared, the dressed terraces of slate drop away,
buttressing the silence.

Below, clouds are mirrored
in the penetrating deep blue eye of a still lake.

2.
Blasting the slate from ancient faces,
cleaving the flat slabs,

the quarrymen split fragments
of themselves, and loosening

uncanny emanations, felt the abrupt
resonance of bone and bone.

3.

Now, beneath the same blue slate
they once worked, the quarrymen

rest. Earth breaks down their substance
into earth. Alike impassive, remote,

cemetery and quarry
reciprocate a durable repose.
