Low Moor Lad


In this unusual memoir of his first eleven years, Richard Poole writes about his family, friends and early schooling in what he recalls as an idyllic semi-urban, semi-rural setting. It was an area he roamed, alone or with friends, with a freedom almost unknown to twenty-first century British children, and one he came to know like the back of his hand. As a result, the environment of the village with its streets, shops, park, lakes, reservoir, fields, farms and woodlands emerges virtually as a character in its own right - a character now dead and gone after the old village was demolished around 1970.

Whilst the major part of Low Moor Lad is written in prose, this book also contains the poem sequence 'Childhood sonnets'. It closes with a poem expressing the shock the author felt on first encountering, unprepared, the ruins of the village he knew and loved.

Low Moor Lad contains 18 photographs.
